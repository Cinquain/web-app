Finding Tesla's Deathplace

There are two men who have profoundly affected my character. The first is Gustave Lebon, a french polymath, the second is Nikola Tesla. I have lived in New York City for a combined period of about ten years, yet I never knew that Nikola Tesla's deathplace was a mile walk away from the place I grab coffee every morning. One day, as I walked near Penn Station, I noticed a small plaque on a wall.



Finding this plaque gave me a combination of intense joy and intense disappointment. It was great to spot the death-place of my childhood hero, but I was so disappointed with how small this memorial was. Considering what Nikola Tesla has done for the collective, we should have a statue of gold erected for this man at the site where he passed away.


Nevertheless, feeling out of breath from excitement, I aimlessly walked around the hotel. I saw the hotel entrance and spotted two doormen standing and being cordial. I started chatting with them and mentioned that I saw the plaque on the wall informing that Tesla had died at this hotel. Yes! The doorman answered, he died at room 3327.



After a casual five minutes chatter with the two doormen, one of them informed me that there is a basement inside the hotel dedicated to Nikola Tesla, a sort of memorial for the great giant. He encouraged me to go take a look and mentioned that it was free to see. My eyes immediately opened, and I quickly jumped on that opportunity. I didn't even think twice.

I walked inside the lobby and headed straight towards the stairs. As I walk in the direction for downstairs, a hotel lobby attendent stops me and asked what was I searching for. I asked him if there was a Tesla memorial downstairs and he promplty answered yes. 'I'm going there', I told him.


As I attempt to continue my journey towards the stairs, the lobby attendent stops me again and tells me about this new resort the New York Hotel just opened. He tells me tell they are looking for individuals to try out the resort in order to get some buzz going. He ask me if I would like to try it, it would be free at no cost and I would receive dinner on the house.


At this moment, the hotel attendent is becoming a nuissance more than anything. I tell him I came here to see the Nikola Tesla memorial and perhaps afterwards I will listen to his pitch. I promptly head downstairs without waiting for his answer and LO! It all became a magical moment.



I felt like I was at a magical place. Here stands this statue of Tesla (not of gold), there are photos of Tesla, images that appear to be his patent drawings, etc. I immediately felt a buzz. I start taking photos and begin doing my job as a scout- dropping this secret spot on the CityXcape app As I post this secret spot on CityXcape, I notice the hotel attendent from upstairs walking in. As I am getting my historical fill, I speak to him about my job as a scout. He becomes fascinated by this exploration lifestyle I tell him and wants me to try the resort even more.


After a twenty minutes chatter with the hotel attendant, I accept his offer for dinner and an experience at his resort. I was rather skeptical of the long thing, but I figured why not give it a try. Turns out the deal was real however. Not only had I found the deathplace of my hero but I got free travel goodies for doing so. This is what scout life is about, getting reward for just living your passion. That's it for this mission guys. Scout Life!
