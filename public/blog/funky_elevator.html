NY's Funkiest Elevator

Having lived in California for the past ten years, coming back to New York felt out of place. The New York I left was no longer present. Something was different... I sensed New York City had become corporate.


As I started my scouting career in the city, I began to discover the old cultural elements I felt was missing in this new version of New York. This secret spot I am about to share with you is one of those things that reminds me how wildly creative and gritty the old New York was. I call it the funkadeli elevator.



When I first walked inside the elevator, I was completely blown away by the craftsmanship. As a hobbyist carpenter myself, I have the eyes and mental insight to appreciate great dexterity and mechanical abilities. The ceiling of the elevator has these strange faces staring at you from up above. Nice combination of creepy yet artistic.



Outside is also a giant arm and hand made out of a schoolbus. It turns out this entire artpiece, including the interior of the elevator was made out of junk metal. Can you imagine how creative and dexterical the mind behind this must be?



As I walked around the elevator, I began asking myself some questions. Who built this? Why? What is the story behind this? ... I decided to investigate! Your job as a scout is not just finding secret spots, you have get the story behind them. Being a scout to the bone, I decided to track down the creator.


I began talking to security, doormen, janitors, building employees, anyone I can stop for chatter and interrogation. I finally get told that the Facilities General Manager of the building across possess the number of the artist I seek to track down. I waste no time and walk to the building facing the one I'm in. I boldly walk to security and ask him for the facilities GM, my tone was confident. Ironically, the GM was standing right near having a standup meeting with his team. The security guard points him to me and I head over there.


I confidently walk to the GM, told him I was a scout, and that I was looking for the artist who created the funky elevator across the street. I let him know that my intel told me he had the artist's number. The GM calmly looks at me, pulls out his phone and gives me the artist's phone number. I couldn't believe it my timing!



Getting the artist's phone number was one thing, but actually making him respond to my phone calls and texts was another. I text him several times and tells him that I am a scout, looking to do a story on his elevator... No Success!



In one of my other scout missions where I found Nikola Tesla's deathplace , I mentioned getting an offer for a free resort night as an experiment the hotel I was scouting was experimenting with. Well it turns out that the person who was processing me for this free night at the hotel was friends with the artist who built the elevator... insane coincidence, I know!


As the man processing my free night at the hotel asked what I did for work, I told him I was a scout and showed him the elevator I was trying to get a story on. He casually said, oh I know the guy who built this, he's a good friend of mine. My eyes opened up! I couldn't believe it.


A bit baffled by the coincidence, I asked the man if he could text his friend on my behalf and get me an interview I've been begging for quite some time. He agreed to do so but told me there would be no promises. He texted JJ (the artist) later that day, and I got the interview later that same week.
